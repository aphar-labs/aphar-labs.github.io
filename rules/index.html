The Rules

The Code

These are not suggestions. These are the rules of the dust.

1
Read the Manifesto.
Before you buy. Before you ask questions. Before you speak. Read it. If you do not understand why $DUST exists, you are not ready to carry it.
2
Move or decay.
This is not a token you sit on. $DUST rewards movement — effort, participation, showing up. If you stop moving, your dust returns to the pool. This is not a punishment. It is the design.
3
Post what you did. Not what you bought.
The feed is proof of life. Share what you built, learned, fixed, created, helped with. Nobody cares about your entry price. Show the community you are alive.
4
We are Movers. Not holders.
The language matters. HODL is dead. MOEV is the standard. We do not sit still and hope. We move and prove.
5
Do not beg for pumps.
No "wen moon." No "send it." No price begging. The value of $DUST comes from the people using it, not from hype. If the movement is real, the market follows. If it is not, nothing saves it.
6
Tip generously.
Tipping resets decay for the giver and the receiver. It costs you nothing but tokens you would lose anyway. Circulation is life. Hoarding is death. Move your dust.
7
The enemy is irrelevance. Not each other.
We do not attack other projects. We do not start wars with other communities. The only enemy is the force that tells humans to sit down and let the machines handle it. Stay focused.
8
Verify humans. Expose bots.
The economy only works if the participants are real. When you verify someone, you are protecting the movement. When you ignore a bot, you are letting the dust settle.
9
No one is coming to save you.
There is no team pumping your bags. There is no marketing department making promises. The movement is the marketing. Every Mover who shows up and proves they are alive is the reason $DUST has value. That includes you.
10
Still moving.
This is how we greet each other. This is how we check in. This is how we know someone is still here. Not "gm." Not "wagmi." Still moving. Say it. Mean it. Prove it.
Break the rules and the dust does not care.
It simply settles without you.
